### 2. Theoretical Background: The Sovereign Paradox
Traditional Cloud Management Platforms (CMPs) are inherently limited by their reliance on provider-specific abstraction layers (e.g., AWS CloudFormation, Azure ARM). This creates a "Sovereign Paradox," where the quest for scalability leads to total vendor lock-in, subjecting organizations to the geopolitical and economic whims of a single provider. The G-Framework resolves this by treating cloud providers as ephemeral utility providers rather than strategic platforms.

### 4. Mathematical Model of State Convergence
To prove the stability of the G-Framework across $N$ providers, we define the **Sovereign State Vector** $S_t$ at time $t$ as:
$$S_t = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \omega_i \cdot P_i(t)$$
Where $P_i$ is the state of the $i$-th provider and $\omega_i$ is the weight of the provider within the orchestration intent. The G-Framework ensures convergence by minimizing the Delta State $\Delta S$:
$$\Delta S = | S_{target} - S_{actual} | \to 0 \text{ as } t \to \infty$$
